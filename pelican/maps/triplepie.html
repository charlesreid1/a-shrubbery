Population is indicated by . One additional person indicates a population increase of one order of magnitude. The people icons are logarithmic!


The basis is the smallest county, Alpine County, which has about 500 commuters. Since log(500) is about 6, we can subtract 5 from the log of every county's population, and this gives us a good indicator, starting at 1, of the order of magnitude of that county's population.

Turn into populations with the formula:

Population = exp( 5 + )

While rounding logs leads to gross errors, like San Bernadino County getting 9 people, for a population of exp(5 + 9) ~ 1.2 M, which is way off from its actual population of 0.7 M, but is closer to 1.2 M (logarithmically speaking) than the next lowest interval, which is exp(5 + 8) ~ 0.4 M.

This is a good illustration of the problem of population data and how multiscale it is: if we had one person represent 1,000 people, Los Angeles County would require 1,000 pages of little people.
